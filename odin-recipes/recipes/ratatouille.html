Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a vibrant and hearty dish that embodies the essence of summer vegetables. Originating from Provence, this colorful medley of eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and tomatoes is gently simmered with aromatic herbs, resulting in a dish that's both comforting and bursting with flavor.

It can be served warm, at room temperature, or even cold, making it a versatile choice for any meal. Whether enjoyed on its own or as a side dish, ratatouille showcases the beauty of fresh ingredients and is a celebration of rustic French cuisine.

INGREDIENTS

  • Eggplant
  • Zucchini
  • Bell peppers
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil
  • Thyme
  • Salt
  • Pepper

STEPS

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Dice the eggplant and sprinkle with salt; let it sit for about 30 minutes to draw out moisture, then rinse and pat dry.
  3. Chop the zucchini, bell peppers, and onion into similar-sized pieces.
  4. Mince the garlic.
  5. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  6. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  7. Add the eggplant and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Add the bell peppers and zucchini, cooking until they start to soften.
  9. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  10. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  12. Let it cool slightly before serving.
